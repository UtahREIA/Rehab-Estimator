Build Scope AI — How the Subscription & Reminder Works

A plain-English breakdown of every step, from purchase to SMS reminder.

Part 1 — When Someone Buys a Subscription
1
GHL
Customer purchases on GHL
The customer buys the Build Scope AI subscription through your GHL payment/funnel page. GHL detects the successful payment and fires a webhook — basically an automatic notification sent to your Vercel server.
↓
2
Vercel API
Your server receives the webhook at /api/subscribe
GHL sends the customer's phone, name, and email to your Vercel server. Your server automatically calculates:

• Expiry Date = today + 1 month
• Reminder Date = expiry date − 5 days
↓
3
Airtable
A record is saved in Airtable
Your server creates (or updates) a row in the "Rehab Estimator Subscribers" Airtable table with:

• Phone Number, Name, Email
• Subscription Status = Active
• Expiring Date (1 month from today)
• Reminder Date (5 days before expiry)
↓
4
Vercel API → GHL
The contact is enrolled in the reminder workflow
Your server calls the GHL API to:

1. Find or create the contact in GHL using their phone number
2. Enroll that specific contact in your reminder workflow
3. Tell GHL: "start this workflow on [Reminder Date]"

GHL then holds the workflow and waits silently until that date. No action is needed from you.
Part 2 — When the Reminder Date Arrives (5 Days Before Expiry)
5
Automatic
GHL fires the workflow on its own
On the reminder date (5 days before expiry), GHL automatically wakes up and runs the workflow for that contact. You don't do anything — GHL handles the scheduling.
↓
6
GHL
First SMS sent — "expires in 5 days"
The workflow runs its first Send SMS action. GHL already knows the contact (from step 4), so it sends to the right number automatically.

"Hi {{contact.first_name}}, your Build Scope AI subscription expires in 5 days. Renew here to keep your access: [link]"
↓
7
Automatic
GHL waits 5 days
The workflow pauses for 5 days. During this time the subscriber may or may not renew. GHL just waits — no action needed from you.
↓
8
GHL
If/Else check — did they renew?
GHL checks the contact's Rehab Subscription Status custom field:

• = Active → they renewed → workflow ends, no more messages ✅
• = Inactive / anything else → they didn't renew → send expiry SMS ❌

This field is automatically updated by your server — Active on subscribe, Inactive on cancel/expiry.
↓
9
GHL
Second SMS sent — "subscription has expired"
Only sent if the If/Else determined they did not renew.

"Hi {{contact.first_name}}, your Build Scope AI subscription has now expired. You no longer have access to the Rehab Estimator. Renew here to get back in: [link]"
Part 3 — When Someone Accesses the Calculator
10
Vercel API
User enters their phone number on the gate screen
When someone opens the Rehab Estimator, they see a phone number gate. They enter their phone and click "Unlock Estimator." Your server checks the Airtable table for that phone number.
↓
11
Airtable
Server checks Subscription Status
• Status = Active → access granted, calculator unlocks ✅
• Status = Inactive → access denied, shown renewal message ❌
• Not found → access denied, shown subscribe message ❌
Part 4 — When Someone Cancels
12
GHL
Cancellation triggers another webhook
When a customer cancels, GHL fires a webhook to /api/cancel-subscription. Your server finds their record in Airtable and sets:

• Subscription Status = Inactive
• Subscription Cancelled = true
• Expiring Date = today (immediate cut-off)

Next time they try to access the calculator, they'll be denied.
Why GHL Knows Who to Send the SMS To
The key thing to understand: Your Vercel server doesn't just save data — it also calls the GHL API directly to find/create the contact AND enroll them in the workflow by their GHL Contact ID. So by the time GHL runs the workflow, it already has the contact linked. The workflow doesn't need to "find" anyone — the contact is already attached.
🔗 Think of it like this...

Imagine you hand a letter to a courier and say "deliver this to John Smith at 123 Main St on April 20th." The courier doesn't need to look up John's address on the day — you already told them. GHL works the same way: your server says "enroll Contact ID abc123 in this workflow, starting April 20th." GHL just executes it on that date.

Your GHL Workflow — What It Looks Like
📋 Your workflow has 4 actions total

Because all the complexity (finding the contact, calculating dates, scheduling) is handled by your Vercel server code, the GHL workflow stays clean:

No trigger needed — enrollment comes from the API, not a GHL event.
No Find Contact needed — the contact is already attached on enrollment.


Action 1: Send SMS — "expires in 5 days, renew here"
Action 2: Wait — 5 days
Action 3: If/Else — Rehab Subscription Status = Active?
    → Yes (Branch): End Workflow
    → No (None): Send SMS — "subscription has expired, renew here"
